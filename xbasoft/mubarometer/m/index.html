Tips & tricks

Pressure/altitude indicators

For quick switching between pressure/altitude indicators just tap on the indicator icon.

You can measure the relative altitude. Just tap on the altitude indicator text (i.e. "Abs.") and it will show the relative altitude from the current point. Tap once more (on the "Rel." text) to return back to the absolute values.

The altitude value are based on the current pressure value. That's why it is not so accurate.

The data logger

To collect data μBarometer runs a small service which saves the pressure values.

FAQ

Why does μBarometer not collect data?

There are several reasons:

  • Doze mode - μBarometer can't collect data when a device is in Doze mode
  • Some kind of task killers/battery savers can kill μBarometer. Exclude μBarometer from the list of killed apps
  • Some tools like Zapper Task Killer, Clean Master, Greenify or any Antivirus software can kill μBarometer
It μBarometer stopped to collect data try to change the tracking interval or just turn off and turn on again tracking. Also you can put on the home screen any widget - it tries to restart logger if it needed.

Why is μBarometer show a notification?

Beginning with Android 8.0 (Oreo), Google changed the way you’re notified about apps running in the background on your phone. An application which runs background processes should sent a notification to users. μBarometer runs a such process for tracking air pressure data.

Can I turn off the notification?

You cann't turn off the notification, but can hide it:

  1. Press and hold on the notification.
  2. Turn the notification off.
  3. Tap Done.

The μBarometer app continues to run the tracking process but won't send the notification.

If you hide the μBarometer notification, you’ll receive a different notification from the Android letting you know the μBarometer app is using battery resources. All apps that run in the background affect your phone’s battery life. You can hide this notification as well.
